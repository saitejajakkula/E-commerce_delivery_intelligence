# DeliveryIQ — E-Commerce Delivery Performance Analysis

A consulting-style Business Analyst project focused on identifying operational inefficiencies, delivery delays, courier performance gaps, and revenue risk across a large-scale e-commerce delivery network.

---

# 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes **5,000 e-commerce orders (Jan–Dec 2023)** to uncover the root causes behind high delivery delays and their business impact.

The dashboard simulates a real-world executive reporting environment designed for:

* CEOs
* Operations Heads
* Supply Chain Teams
* Business Analysts
* Strategy Consultants

The objective was to transform raw operational data into strategic business recommendations backed by data storytelling and KPI-driven analysis.

---

# 🚨 Business Problem

The analysis revealed that:

* **49.5% of orders breached the 5-day SLA**
* Delay performance is **3× worse than the industry benchmark**
* Delays are causing:

  * Revenue leakage
  * Customer dissatisfaction
  * Higher cancellation risk
  * Lower repeat purchases

The issue is not isolated to one courier or region — it is a **systemic operational failure**.

# 🔍 Key Insights

## 1. Critical Delay Crisis

* Nearly **1 in 2 orders** misses SLA commitments
* Delay rate far exceeds the 15% industry benchmark

## 2. Courier Performance Gap

| Courier      | Delay Rate |
| ------------ | ---------- |
| India Post   | 57.3%      |
| Ecom Express | 43.7%      |

* Performance gap: **13.6 percentage points**
* Current routing logic ignores courier efficiency

## 3. Regional Underperformance

| Region | Delay Rate |
| ------ | ---------- |
| North  | 55.8%      |
| West   | 42.9%      |

* North India is the weakest-performing region
* Delhi and Lucknow emerged as high-risk operational zones

## 4. Seasonal Demand Failures

Peak delay months:

* January → 59.5%
* September → 56.3%
* July → 53.8%

This indicates poor capacity planning during demand spikes.

## 5. Revenue at Risk

Estimated annual revenue exposure:

* **₹18–21 Crores**

Delayed customers are:

* More likely to cancel
* Less likely to reorder
* Higher churn risk

---

# 🧠 Root Cause Analysis

A structured 5-Why analysis identified the following root causes:

1. High SLA breach rate
2. Courier inefficiency
3. No SLA-linked penalty system
4. Poor seasonal capacity planning
5. No real-time monitoring infrastructure

The organization currently operates reactively instead of proactively.

---

# ✅ Strategic Recommendations

## 1. SLA Contract Reform

Introduce courier penalty clauses tied to delay performance.

## 2. Smart Courier Routing

Route high-value orders through top-performing couriers.

## 3. Peak Season Capacity Planning

Add operational buffer capacity before demand spikes.

## 4. Real-Time SLA Monitoring

Implement rolling delay dashboards and automated alerts.

## 5. Regional Operations Audit

Conduct deep operational audits in North India.

## 6. Proactive Customer Communication

Automated delay notifications and compensation workflows.
